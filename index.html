Research & projects

Clusterwords 2026 – Present

A controlled semantic-partition game (Connections-style) to study how humans and LLMs decide when they know enough to commit to an answer under ambiguity. The board generator treats difficulty as a manipulated variable (structure, semantic margin, distractor type), separating combinatorial from semantic difficulty. The live platform now collects matched human and AI play for process-level comparison — candidate sets, confidence, and commitment.

Gentags: Discrete Semantic State for Constraint-Sensitive Decision Pipelines 2025 – 2026

First-author preprint introducing Gentags, a representation that compresses source text into short, evidence-grounded semantic units (1–4 words, each isolating one identifiable condition) used as inspectable, structured intermediate state in LLM decision pipelines. In a controlled study isolating representation structure (fixed evidence, task, and judge), Gentags raised agreement with full-evidence decisions to 79.5% (vs. 52.3–61.6% for RAKE/YAKE/TF-IDF) and hard-constraint satisfaction to 97.3% (vs. 84.7–89.3%), with high cross-run stability.
