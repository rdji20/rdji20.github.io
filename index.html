Research & projects

Clusterwords 2026 – Present

A controlled environment for studying decision-making under uncertainty, built around a Connections-style puzzle: a grid of words hides several secret groups, and the task is to find them all before running out of lives. Humans and AI agents play the exact same boards, so each match becomes a process-level comparison of how a mind moves from ambiguous information to a committed answer — candidate sets, confidence, and the moment of commitment. Board difficulty is manipulated along two independent axes, separating combinatorial difficulty (board size, from a 2×2 probe up to 5×5) from semantic difficulty (structure, semantic margin, distractor type). Boards are machine-generated to reduce the chance a given puzzle already appeared in a model's training data. The underlying interest is the cognitive control behind any hard call: surfacing options, holding them in working memory, inhibiting the obvious-but-wrong group, and knowing when you are actually sure.

Gentags: Discrete Semantic State for Constraint-Sensitive Decision Pipelines 2025 – 2026

First-author preprint introducing Gentags, a representation that compresses source text into short, evidence-grounded semantic units (1–4 words, each isolating one identifiable condition) used as inspectable, structured intermediate state in LLM decision pipelines. In a controlled study isolating representation structure (fixed evidence, task, and judge), Gentags raised agreement with full-evidence decisions to 79.5% (vs. 52.3–61.6% for RAKE/YAKE/TF-IDF) and hard-constraint satisfaction to 97.3% (vs. 84.7–89.3%), with high cross-run stability. Dynamic Information 2024

An LLM agent that constructs and self-updates a knowledge/decision graph, with an explicit hypothesis structure (probabilities over hypotheses) and a controlled decision space for Bayesian-style reasoning (Python, Flask, D3.js). Used Connections-style puzzles as a testbed for whether explicit, external uncertainty improves an agent's decisions; the low performance here motivated the shift to studying commitment under uncertainty directly.
