Research

I work at the boundary between computational cognitive science and machine intelligence. Language-based AI systems generate fluent text but do not maintain a stable internal state, so their decisions can be plausible yet inconsistent. My research asks whether making semantic representation, memory dynamics, and belief explicit and updatable lets these systems decide more like reasoning agents and less like fluent guessers.

The through-line is state: making decision-relevant information explicit and addressable, then studying how it supports revision, aggregation, and uncertainty over time. I care about work that is both cognitively motivated and empirically testable.

  • Structured, discrete semantic state for language models
  • Context and memory dynamics during inference
  • Uncertainty, revision, and decision consistency
  • Cognitive models of mind as testable machine architectures
